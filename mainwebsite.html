There were many countries that were once communist, such as Russia, Ukraine, Mongolia, Azerbaijan, and many more. What is Communism?

Definition: Communism is a theory or system of social organization in which all property is owned by the community and each person contributes and receives according to their ability and needs.

Source of the definition

How it all started: Communism was an economic-political philosophy founded by Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels in the second half of the 19th century. Marx and Engels met in 1844, and discovered that they had similar principles. In 1848 they wrote and published "The Communist Manifesto." They desired to end capitalism feeling that it was the social class system that led to the exploitation of workers, and therefore started their own political system.

The Pros of Communism

  1. Communism has a centrally planned economy, giving it the ability to quickly mobilize economic resources on a large scale, and creating more industrial power.
  2. Communism can also move effectively because it overrides individual self-interest, and instead subjugates the welfare of the general population to achieve imperative social goals.
  3. Because the goal of communism is to improve the society as a whole, many individuals are sent through advanced schooling, so that their skill sets can be very enhanced.

The Cons of Communism

  1. The most significant disadvantage of Communism is that it eliminates the free market, and that instead of the laws of supply and demand setting prices, the government does.
  2. There are also specific caps placed on the amount of income that can be earned by individuals, which makes it difficult for innovation to grow, as there is no motivation to work harder.
  3. The goal of Communism is to have people working toward common goals and needs. For that reason, people are not always free to pursue their own interests, and that job assignments are based on what the community requires.
